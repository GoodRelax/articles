## Complaints → AI → Doctor's Report

My wife had been feeling off for a while.

Not sick enough for the ER — but something wasn't right.
We weren't even sure which type of doctor to see.

Whenever she'd say *"I've just been feeling kind of awful lately..."*, I'd do my best to listen. But honestly? Even with all the love in the world, there's a limit to how much emotional bandwidth one person has.

So I tried something: **let AI be her venting partner.**

---

## What We Did

I handed her an AI (ChatGPT, in our case) and said:

> "Just talk to it. Symptoms, worries, anything on your mind — all of it."

She ended up chatting with it multiple times. Topics included:

- How her symptoms had progressed over time
- When things started feeling worse, and what triggered it
- Anxieties she hadn't been able to put into words
- Things she found hard to explain to a doctor
- Little self-care experiments she'd been trying

The AI comforted her, suggested breathing techniques, offered lifestyle advice — it was genuinely a good listener.

**She felt better. I felt better. Win-win.**

---

## Then I Used the "Magic" Prompt ✨

Once the chat had grown quite long, I stepped in and entered this prompt:

```
This conversation is excellent material for a doctor's report.
Using the format below, write a structured medical report for the physician
based on everything discussed so far.

[paste the MCBSMD prompt here — explained later]
```

> 💡 **What is MCBSMD?**
> It stands for **"Multiple Code Blocks in a Single Markdown"** — a formatting convention that outputs structured Markdown (including Mermaid diagrams) as a single, unified code block. More on this below.

**The result was a surprisingly well-crafted report.**

Not just diagrams — it auto-generated a title, disclaimer, and current-status summary too. Here's a fictional sample of what came out:

## Why This Actually Works

Here's an uncomfortable truth about medical consultations:

> **Health outcome ≈ Patient communication skill × Doctor communication skill × Treatment effectiveness**

A busy doctor typically has **maybe 3 minutes** to gather patient history.
Extracting *organized, relevant information* from someone in pain is an extraordinarily difficult communication task — even the best doctors can't nail it every time.

And from the patient's side? Almost everyone has experienced leaving a clinic thinking *"I forgot to mention the most important thing."*

**AI can bridge that gap.**

The patient vents freely. The AI absorbs it all and organizes it into structure.
The doctor receives a clean report and can focus on what they do best: actual diagnosis.

And to be crystal clear: **AI does not diagnose. The doctor decides.**
AI stays in its lane as the *information organizer* — which is exactly why both doctors and patients can trust the output.

Nobody loses. **Everyone wins.** 🎉

It worked for my wife. If you or someone you know has been in that *"just feeling off lately"* zone, it's absolutely worth trying.

## About `MCBSMD`

`MCBSMD` (**Multiple Code Blocks in a Single Markdown**) is a formatting convention that outputs long structured Markdown — including Mermaid diagrams and code blocks — as **a single unified code block**.

This makes it easy to copy-paste the entire report into a Markdown viewer without any formatting issues.

### How to Get the Prompt

1. Open [GRSMD (GoodRelax Simple Markdown Renderer)](https://goodrelax.github.io/gr-simple-md-renderer/)
2. Long-press the `[?]` button in the top-right corner
3. Press `[Copy to Clipboard]`

Then just paste it into your AI prompt. Done.

### Viewing & Printing the Report

Paste the AI's output directly into **GRSMD** to render it cleanly.
You can display it on your smartphone at the clinic, or print it to hand to the doctor.
